About

My present research area is biomedical informatics, where I concentrate on using clinical patient data to indentify latent dynamical properties within the patient+healthcare process. This interdisciplinary work sits at the nexus of medicine, data science, dynamical systems, and physiology.
I previously worked in geophysics (specifically, Arctic oceanography, climate, and sea-ice) with a research emphasis on numerical modeling of dynamical processes and data assimilation via variational, ensemble-based methods.
Before that, I studied mathematics/physics and taught 100+ credit-hours of undergraduate mathematics (2007--2016).
At heart, I'm an applied mathematician rather than a scientist. My interests lie in mathematics and natural systems.
Long-term opportunities that better align with my interests are considered.

  • Researcher (current), Biomedical Informatics, University of Colorado School of of Medicine
  • Postdoc (past), Biomedical Engineering, University of Colorado Denver | Anschutz
  • PhD, Interdiciplinary, University of Alaska Fairbanks
